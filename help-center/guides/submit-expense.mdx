## Overview

Submit out-of-pocket expenses with a receipt so Grantmaster can extract details automatically, route the expense to the right project or grant, and send it for approval.

Expenses you submit feed into project and grant budgets, and into financial reporting and compliance.

<Callout kind="info">

Prerequisites:

- You are added as a member on the relevant project or grant.
- You have a clear receipt image or PDF ready to upload.
- You know any required fields your organization enforces, such as project, grant, category, date, and amount.

If you are missing access to a project or grant, contact your administrator before submitting.

</Callout>

## Submit an expense

Use these steps each time you need to submit an expense with a receipt.

<Steps>

<Step title="Create a new expense" icon="plus">

- Open Grantmaster and go to the expenses area from the main navigation.
- Start a new expense using the generic add or new action.
- Choose whether this is a personal reimbursement or a project expense if asked.

You should now see an empty expense form with basic fields like date, merchant, amount, and project or grant.

</Step>

<Step title="Select the project or grant" icon="folder">

- In the project or grant field, search for and select the project or grant that funded the expense.
- If your organization tracks both, choose the correct combination (for example, a project under a specific grant).
- If you do not see the right option, cancel the expense for now and ask your administrator to add or assign you to that project or grant.

Linking the expense to the correct project or grant ensures it appears in the right budget and reports.

</Step>

<Step title="Upload your receipt" icon="upload">

- Find the receipt upload area on the expense form.
- Upload your receipt as an image or PDF from your device, or take a photo if your device supports it.
- Wait a few seconds while Grantmaster runs OCR to extract details such as date, merchant, and total.

If the upload fails, the receipt is too large, or the format is not accepted, see [Troubleshoot receipt upload issues](/help-center/troubleshooting/receipt-upload).

</Step>

<Step title="Review OCR-extracted details" icon="search">

- Check that the date, merchant name, currency, and total amount match what is shown on the receipt.
- Confirm that tax, tip, and line item amounts (if extracted) look correct.
- Correct any fields that were misread or left blank, such as unclear totals or non-standard merchant names.

Your changes override the OCR values, and Grantmaster saves the corrected details with the receipt.

</Step>

<Step title="Categorize and allocate the expense" icon="settings">

- Choose the appropriate expense category, such as travel, supplies, or meals, following your organization’s rules.
- If the expense covers multiple projects or grants, split the allocation by percentage or amount according to your internal policy.
- Add any required tags or funding codes your finance team uses for reporting.

Include a short description or purpose, such as the event, trip, or activity the expense supported.

</Step>

<Step title="Add notes and compliance details" icon="edit">

- Add a note that explains why the expense was necessary for the project or grant (for example, “field visit travel for Study A”).
- Include participant counts, locations, or agenda references when policies require them.
- Attach any supporting documents besides the receipt, such as approvals, agendas, or contracts, if your organization expects them.

Clear notes help your manager and finance team approve the expense without delays.

</Step>

<Step title="Submit the expense for approval" icon="check-circle">

- Review all fields one last time, including the selected project or grant, total amount, and category.
- Confirm the attached receipt is clear and legible when you open the preview.
- Submit the expense using the standard submit or send for approval action.

After submission, the expense status should change from draft to pending approval, and your manager or designated approver is notified.

</Step>

</Steps>

<Callout kind="tip">

For faster approvals:

- Submit expenses soon after the transaction date, not in large batches at month end.
- Use high-quality receipt images with full totals and dates visible.
- Add concise but specific notes that answer who, what, when, where, and why.

</Callout>

## What happens after you submit

Once you submit an expense, Grantmaster routes it to the appropriate approver based on your organization’s workflow, usually your direct manager or project lead.

Approvers see the expense details, receipt image or PDF, allocations, and your notes in a single view so they can confirm the expense is valid and correctly coded.

### What managers and approvers check

Managers and approvers typically look for:

- The expense is linked to the correct project or grant.
- The amount, date, and merchant on the form match the receipt.
- The category, allocations, and tags follow internal policies.
- The notes explain the business purpose clearly.
- The expense falls within any budget or per-diem limits.

They then either approve the expense, which moves it into reporting and reimbursement workflows, or reject or send it back with comments.

### If your expense is rejected or needs changes

If an approver rejects your expense or requests changes, Grantmaster records their comments and returns the expense to you.

- Open the expense from your expenses list and review the approver’s message.
- Edit the fields they flagged, such as category, project or grant, amount, or notes.
- Replace the receipt if they mention that the image is unclear or incomplete.
- Resubmit the expense for approval.

The status should update again to pending approval, and the approver will see the updated version with your corrections.

## How expenses feed reporting and compliance

After approval, Grantmaster includes the expense in project and grant budgets, financial reports, and audit trails.

Linking receipts, allocations, and notes to each expense helps your organization demonstrate that funds were used appropriately for each project or grant.
