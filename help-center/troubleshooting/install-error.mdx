---
title: Grantmaster page will not load
description: Fix Grantmaster pages that show a blank screen, spinner, or unexpected error by checking browser cache, network access, session, and org.
---

## Symptoms

- Grantmaster opens to a blank screen or endless loading spinner.
- A specific page (for example, Dashboard or Grants) shows an unexpected error message.
- Other websites work, but Grantmaster pages are slow or partially loaded.
- You recently switched orgs or roles and the page stopped loading as expected.

<Callout kind="info">

These steps apply to the Grantmaster web app in any modern browser (Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari) on desktop or laptop devices.

</Callout>

## Fix

<Steps>
  <Step title="Refresh the page and try a hard reload" icon="refresh-cw">
    Use a normal refresh first. If the page is still blank or broken, try a hard reload to bypass cached files:

    - On Windows/Linux: press <kbd>Ctrl</kbd>+<kbd>Shift</kbd>+<kbd>R</kbd> in your browser.
    - On macOS: press <kbd>Command</kbd>+<kbd>Shift</kbd>+<kbd>R</kbd>.

    If Grantmaster loads correctly after a hard reload, the issue was likely stale cached assets in your browser.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Try an incognito or private window" icon="monitor">
    Open a new incognito or private browsing window and sign in to Grantmaster from there.

    - If pages load correctly in incognito, an extension or cached data in your main profile is likely interfering.
    - Temporarily disable ad blockers, script blockers, or privacy extensions for the Grantmaster domain, then retry in a normal window.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check your network and blocked scripts" icon="wifi">
    Confirm you have a stable internet connection and that your network is not blocking parts of the app.

    - Try loading another secure site that uses dynamic content, such as a dashboard or email app.
    - If you are on a corporate network or VPN, ask your IT team to confirm that Grantmaster is allowed and that no scripts or API calls to Grantmaster are blocked by a firewall or proxy.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify you are signed in and in the correct org" icon="users">
    Confirm that your session is active and that you have access to the org you expect.

    - Check that your user avatar or initials are visible in the top bar and that the org name matches the org where you expect the page to work.
    - If you recently switched orgs or changed roles (ADMIN, MANAGER, MEMBER, AUDITOR), switch back to the correct org and retry the page.
    - If you suspect a sign in issue, follow the steps in [Cannot sign in to Grantmaster](/help-center/troubleshooting/cant-sign-in) and then try loading the page again.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check for an outage or maintenance" icon="alert-triangle">
    Ask your admin or IT team whether your org has a status page or internal communications channel for app outages.

    - If a status page exists, check whether Grantmaster is affected.
    - If you see a reported outage or maintenance window, wait for it to resolve and then reload the page.

    <Callout kind="info">

    Some organizations publish a status page for their critical apps. If your team uses one, check it for any open incidents affecting Grantmaster.

    </Callout>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Capture error details and contact support" icon="help-circle">
    If the page still will not load, collect details so support can help you faster.

    - Take a screenshot of the entire browser window, including any visible error message.
    - If you are comfortable with developer tools, open the browser console and note any red error messages or failed network requests when loading the page.
    - Include the page URL, your browser name and version, and your org name when you contact your internal support team or Grantmaster support.

    With these details, support can quickly see whether the issue is specific to your browser, your org access, or a broader incident.
  </Step>
</Steps>
