---

## Symptoms

Receipt upload or OCR issues usually show up as one of these problems:

- Upload fails with an error before the receipt appears on the expense.
- You see a message that the file is too large or the upload stalls.
- The file format is not accepted when you try to attach it.
- The upload spinner runs for a long time and then times out.
- The AI/OCR fills in the wrong merchant, date, or total amount.
- Line items are missing or obviously incomplete after extraction.

Use the steps below to match your symptom and fix it.

<Callout kind="alert">

Grantmaster stores receipts and extracted data under your organization's policies. Avoid uploading personal or unrelated documents that contain sensitive information not needed for reimbursement.

</Callout>

## Fix upload errors

Use this section when the file will not upload, you see "file too large" or "unsupported format", or the upload is very slow.

<Steps>

<Step title="Check supported file types and size" icon="alert-triangle">

Most organizations allow common image and document formats such as `JPG`, `PNG`, and `PDF`. If you try to upload other formats, the upload can fail.

- Convert screenshots or scans from formats like `HEIC`, `WEBP`, or `TIFF` to `JPG` or `PNG`.
- Export documents (for example, from a scanning app) as `PDF` instead of proprietary formats.
- If you see a "file too large" message, reduce the file size below your organization's limit by using a lower-resolution export or a compression tool.

After converting or compressing the file, upload the new version to the expense again.

</Step>

<Step title="Reduce image resolution or crop the receipt" icon="image">

Very high-resolution photos can be slow to upload or hit size limits without looking different on screen.

- Crop the photo so it shows only the receipt, not your desk or background.
- Use your phone's "medium" or "standard" quality setting instead of "maximum".
- Save or export the file again, then attach the smaller version to the expense.

If the upload succeeds after resizing or cropping, you should see a thumbnail of your receipt on the expense.

</Step>

<Step title="Improve your connection and retry" icon="cloud">

Slow or unstable connections can cause uploads to stall or time out, especially for larger PDFs.

- If possible, switch from mobile data to a stable Wi‑Fi connection.
- Close other apps or browser tabs that might be using bandwidth heavily.
- Refresh the expense page, then start the upload again and wait until the thumbnail appears.

If the issue only occurs on one network (for example, guest Wi‑Fi), try another network or a VPN session approved by your organization.

</Step>

<Step title="Try another device or browser" icon="monitor">

Some upload issues are device- or browser-specific.

- If you are on a phone, try uploading the same receipt from a desktop browser.
- If you are on desktop, try a different browser (for example, switch between Chrome, Edge, or Safari).
- If a browser extension blocks uploads, open a private/incognito window and upload the receipt there.

If the receipt uploads correctly on one device but not another, share that detail with your admin or support if you need further help.

</Step>

</Steps>

<Callout kind="tip">

For reliable uploads and better OCR, use clear, uncropped photos in `JPG` or `PNG` under your organization's size limit, or clean, legible `PDF` exports from your scanner.

</Callout>

## Fix incorrect OCR totals, dates, or merchants

Grantmaster uses AI/OCR on uploaded receipts to prefill merchant, date, total, and related fields in the expense. If these values are wrong, you remain responsible for submitting accurate information.

<Steps>

<Step title="Check image quality and retake if needed" icon="camera">

Poor-quality images are the most common reason for bad OCR. Before you rely on extracted values:

- Make sure the entire receipt is visible, including the merchant name, date, taxes, and total.
- Avoid glare, shadows, and reflections on glossy paper.
- Hold the camera parallel to the receipt so the text is not skewed.
- Use good lighting and ensure the text looks sharp when you zoom in.

If the image is hard to read with your eyes, take a new photo and upload it so OCR has a clean source.

</Step>

<Step title="Crop and increase contrast" icon="edit">

Busy backgrounds and extra content around the receipt can confuse OCR.

- Crop the image tightly around the receipt so only the document is visible.
- If your camera or editing app supports it, increase contrast and brightness slightly so text stands out.
- Remove other documents or hands from the frame before retaking the photo.

Upload the updated image to the expense and let OCR run again. You should see more accurate merchant, date, and total fields.

</Step>

<Step title="Manually correct extracted fields" icon="edit">

If OCR still reads the wrong values, always fix them before submission:

- Edit the **merchant**, **date**, **currency**, **tax**, and **total** fields directly in the expense form.
- Add or correct any **line items** the OCR missed, including descriptions, categories, and amounts.
- Verify that the final total in the expense matches the total printed on the receipt.

These edits override the OCR output and are what your approver and finance team will use.

</Step>

<Step title="Explain corrections for your approver" icon="users">

When the receipt is hard to read or the totals are not obvious, adding a short note helps your approver review faster.

- Use the comment or notes field on the expense to explain any differences.
- Mention details like split totals, tips written by hand, or multi-page receipts.
- Call out if you combined several receipts into one expense or left some text untranslated.

Approvers see both your corrected fields and the original receipt image, so clear notes reduce back-and-forth questions.

</Step>

</Steps>

## Fix missing or incomplete line items

Sometimes OCR captures the total but not individual items, taxes, or categories.

<Steps>

<Step title="Confirm that item details are visible" icon="search">

OCR cannot extract line items that are cut off or blurred.

- Check that each item line, including quantity and price, is readable in the uploaded image.
- Ensure page edges are not cut off when you photograph long or multi-page receipts.
- For multi-page receipts, upload all pages so the full list is available.

If details are missing from the photo, retake it to include the full receipt.

</Step>

<Step title="Add or adjust line items manually" icon="plus">

If OCR does not create line items or misses some entries:

- Manually add line items in the expense form with clear descriptions and amounts.
- Group small items where allowed by your policy, such as "Snacks at station" instead of listing each low-value item separately.
- Set the appropriate category for each line so routing and approvals are correct.

The sum of your line items should match the total value on the receipt.

</Step>

<Step title="Use notes for complex receipts" icon="info">

For receipts with many items or unusual layouts, a brief summary is often clearer than one item per line.

- Summarize the key parts that matter for policy, such as "Hotel stay: room, taxes, parking".
- Explain large or exceptional items that might trigger extra review.
- If the receipt includes personal items not being claimed, call out what you excluded.

These details help approvers understand why the total you submitted differs from the printed total, if it does.

</Step>

</Steps>

## When to contact support or your admin

If you have tried the steps above and still cannot upload a receipt or get usable OCR results:

- Capture screenshots or photos that show the error message and the receipt you tried to upload.
- Note whether the problem happens on multiple devices or networks.
- Share this information with your internal admin or support contact so they can investigate or escalate.

Your organization may also have specific rules about accepted formats, maximum file sizes, or when OCR is required versus optional.
