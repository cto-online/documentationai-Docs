## Getting started FAQ

Grantmaster supports end-to-end grant management plus expenses, time tracking, and compliance so your team can manage awards from discovery through closeout.

<ExpandableGroup>

<Expandable title="What is Grantmaster and what does it cover?" default-open="true">

Grantmaster centralizes your full grant lifecycle in one workspace, from finding opportunities through reporting and closeout.

It covers:

- **Grant discovery and intake** so you can track potential opportunities and applications.
- **Award management** including budgets, milestones, and reporting deadlines.
- **Expenses and reimbursements** with receipt OCR and policy checks.
- **Time tracking** via journals to capture effort across projects.
- **Compliance monitoring and audits** with exportable records.

Use it as the system of record for all grant-related work, rather than stitching together email, spreadsheets, and separate finance tools.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Who is Grantmaster for on my team?">

Grantmaster supports several roles so different people see the right level of control and detail:

- **ADMIN** — Owns organization-wide setup: account configuration, policies, integrations, and billing. Admins create projects, define approval flows, and manage user access.
- **MANAGER** — Oversees grants and teams: reviews applications, approves expenses and journals, and monitors progress toward grant deliverables.
- **MEMBER** — Submits work: files expenses with receipts, logs time in journals, and updates tasks or deliverables tied to grants.
- **AUDITOR** — Has read-only access: reviews transactions, journals, and compliance status, and runs exports without changing data.

If you are unsure which role you need, start as a MEMBER and ask an ADMIN to adjust your access based on your responsibilities. For more detail, read [Roles and permissions](/help-center/faq/roles-permissions).

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="How do I get access to Grantmaster?">

You usually get access in one of two ways:

- **Invited by an ADMIN** — Your organization ADMIN adds you with the right role. You receive an email invitation with a sign-in link.
- **Joining an existing workspace** — If your organization already uses Grantmaster, ask an ADMIN or MANAGER to invite you so your work is connected to the right projects.

After you sign in the first time, confirm:

- Your name and email are correct.
- Your role (ADMIN, MANAGER, MEMBER, AUDITOR) matches what you need.
- You see at least one project or grant workspace on your home screen.

If you do not see the right projects, contact an ADMIN to review your access.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Where should I start once I am logged in?">

Where you start depends on your role and what you are trying to accomplish.

- **Admins** — Start by setting up your first grant project, approval rules, and policies.
- **Managers** — Review active grants, open tasks, and pending approvals (expenses or journals).
- **Members** — Go straight to logging time, submitting expenses, or updating deliverables.
- **Auditors** — Open reports and exports to understand current spend, effort, and compliance.

<Callout kind="info">

For a guided first setup, follow [Set up your first project](/help-center/guides/setup-project). It walks Admins and Managers through creating a project, inviting team members, and wiring up approvals.

</Callout>

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="How does Grantmaster support the full grants lifecycle?">

Grantmaster tracks the lifecycle from initial opportunity through final reporting and closeout, so you can see the full story of each grant.

At a high level:

- **Discovery and application** — Capture potential grants and record application status.
- **Award and setup** — Create a project for each funded grant, define budgets and timelines, and assign teams.
- **Delivery and spend** — Log time in journals, submit expenses with receipts, and route items for Manager or Admin approval.
- **Monitoring and compliance** — Compare actuals to budgets, monitor policy compliance, and prepare for audits with exportable data.
- **Reporting and closeout** — Use journals and expenses to support narrative and financial reports, then close out the grant when completed.

For a deeper walkthrough of each stage, see [Grants lifecycle overview](/help-center/faq/grants-lifecycle).

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Where do I track expenses and time for a grant?">

Use two core areas to capture day-to-day work:

- **Expenses** — Submit purchases, travel, and reimbursements against a specific grant or project. Attach receipts and let Grantmaster extract key details with OCR, then route the expense for approval according to your policies.
- **Journals (time tracking)** — Log your time by day, project, and activity so Managers and Admins can see how effort maps to each grant.

Managers and Admins review and approve these items, which keeps spend and effort aligned with each grant budget and reporting requirements.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Where can I get help if I get stuck?">

If you need help:

- **Check the Help Center** — Use the search bar or browse FAQs and guides from the Help Center home.
- **Look for in-app tips** — Many Grantmaster pages include inline help and links back to detailed docs.
- **Ask your Admin** — For access, roles, or policy questions, an ADMIN in your organization is the best first contact.

If you still cannot find an answer, share the exact page and action you are trying to take with your Admin so they can escalate or open a support request with full context.

</Expandable>
