## Grants lifecycle overview

Grantmaster tracks each opportunity through a consistent lifecycle: discovery → pipeline → submitted → won → active grant → reporting → closed. Managers, admins, and members use these stages to keep forecasts, compliance, and reporting accurate.

<Callout kind="info">

Organizations sometimes rename or add stages to match their internal language, but the underlying meanings stay consistent with the lifecycle described here.

</Callout>

<ExpandableGroup>

<Expandable title="What are the standard grant lifecycle stages?">

Grantmaster models a grant lifecycle roughly as: **Discovery → Pipeline → Submitted → Won → Active Grant → Reporting → Closed**. Each stage reflects how committed your organization is and what work you are doing.

- **Discovery** — You have identified a potential opportunity, but you have not committed resources yet.
- **Pipeline** — You are actively working the opportunity (drafting, gathering data, coordinating partners) but have not submitted.
- **Submitted** — You have sent the application to the funder and are waiting for a decision.
- **Won** — The funder has awarded the grant, but setup and onboarding might still be in progress.
- **Active Grant** — You are spending against the grant, logging time/journals, and tracking compliance.
- **Reporting** — The performance or financial period is ending, and your primary work is preparing and submitting reports.
- **Closed** — You have completed spending, reporting, and closeout; the grant is archived as history.

Use these stages consistently so your dashboards reflect real progress from early ideas to closed grants.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="What does the Discovery stage mean?">

Discovery captures **early-stage ideas and leads** before you invest effort. Typical items in this stage include new funding announcements, referrals from partners, or opportunities your team is still evaluating.

Keep opportunities in Discovery while you:

- Decide whether the grant aligns with your strategy.
- Estimate capacity and fit.
- Gather high-level requirements and deadlines.

Move a grant out of Discovery and into Pipeline when someone is clearly responsible for pursuing it and work has started.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="What happens in the Pipeline stage?">

Pipeline represents **active pre-submission work**. Grants in Pipeline should have an owner and a clear path toward submission.

Use Pipeline while you:

- Draft narratives, budgets, and work plans.
- Coordinate with partners and internal teams.
- Collect required documents and approvals.

Because Pipeline opportunities are actively in progress, managers often use this stage for **forecasting** upcoming submissions and workloads. Move the opportunity to Submitted once the application has been formally sent to the funder.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="What does Submitted mean, and when do I use it?">

Submitted means you have **formally sent the application** to the funder and are waiting for a decision. No additional drafting work is happening, but you may still respond to clarifying questions.

Use the Submitted stage to:

- Track how many pending decisions you have.
- Monitor expected decision dates.
- Separate active writing work (Pipeline) from waiting (Submitted).

Move the grant to **Won** when the funder approves it, or handle a declined decision as described in the declined grant question.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="What does Won mean in the lifecycle?">

Won indicates the funder has **approved the application** and intends to fund the grant. At this point you may still be finalizing contracts, compliance reviews, or internal setup.

Keep a grant in Won while you:

- Wait for signed agreements or award letters.
- Confirm the final budget and reporting schedule.
- Set up internal structures such as projects, codes, or cost centers.

After you complete setup and are ready to start spending, move the grant to **Active Grant** so the system ties expenses, time, and compliance tracking to the project.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="What does Active Grant mean?">

Active Grant means the grant has **started**: you can spend funds, log time/journals, and track compliance against it. This is the stage most operational work centers around.

In Active Grant you typically:

- Allocate budgets and assign team members.
- Track expenses, journals/time, and deliverables against the grant or project.
- Monitor compliance requirements and upcoming reporting deadlines.

Reporting tools use Active Grant status to show current grant-funded activity. When the active period ends and you are focused mainly on reports, move the grant into **Reporting**.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="What is the Reporting stage used for?">

Reporting covers the **end-of-period or end-of-grant** phase where your main work is preparing and submitting reports, rather than spending.

Use the Reporting stage when:

- The grant period has ended or is about to end.
- Most spending is complete and you are reconciling expenses.
- You are drafting and submitting narrative or financial reports.

This stage helps managers see which grants are in closeout work, even if no new activity is being charged. After you complete all required reports and receive final acceptance, move the grant into **Closed**.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="When is a grant considered Closed?">

Closed means you have **fully finished** work on the grant:

- The grant period has ended and no new spending is allowed.
- All required reports are submitted and accepted.
- Any compliance or audit follow-ups are resolved.

Closed grants remain available for historical reporting, but they no longer appear in active work queues. Move grants to Closed once managers confirm there is no remaining operational or reporting work.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Who can update a grant&apos;s stage?">

Stage updates depend on your organization&apos;s role settings. Typically:

- **Members** who own a grant move it through Discovery, Pipeline, and Submitted as they work.
- **Managers** or **Admins** confirm transitions into Won, Active Grant, Reporting, and Closed to keep portfolio data consistent.

Check your specific permissions in the [Roles and permissions FAQ](/help-center/faq/roles-permissions). If you cannot change a stage you expect to manage, ask an Admin to adjust your role.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="How does the lifecycle stage affect reporting and visibility?">

Lifecycle stage controls **where a grant appears** and which metrics include it:

- **Discovery and Pipeline** feed pipeline and forecast views but usually do not affect financial actuals.
- **Submitted** opportunities appear in pending-decision dashboards so leadership can monitor upcoming awards.
- **Won and Active Grant** drive most **operational and financial reporting**, including expenses, journals/time, and compliance metrics tied to the grant or project.
- **Reporting** highlights grants in closeout workflows for managers and compliance teams.
- **Closed** grants stay available for history and trend analysis but drop out of active work and alert queues.

If a grant is showing up in the wrong dashboards, verify that the stage accurately reflects its real-world status.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="How should I handle a declined or unsuccessful grant?">

When a funder declines an application, move the opportunity out of Submitted so your pipeline and decision metrics stay accurate.

Common options include:

- Moving the grant directly to **Closed** with a declined reason.
- Using a custom stage such as **Declined** or **Not funded**, if your organization prefers more detailed tracking.

The key is to keep declined opportunities out of Submitted and Won so dashboards only show realistic forecasts and awards. Admins can configure any additional internal stages while preserving the overall lifecycle meaning.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="What does &quot;Active&quot; mean if we are not spending yet?">

Active Grant means the funder has awarded the grant and you are **ready to begin work**, even if the first expenses have not hit yet. Use Active when:

- The grant start date has arrived.
- Internal setup is complete and teams can charge time or expenses.
- You want operational dashboards to treat the grant as live.

If you have a long gap between award and actual activity, you can keep the grant in **Won** until you are ready, then move it to **Active Grant** when teams should start using it in their journals or expense coding.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Can we customize the names or number of stages?">

Yes. Many organizations **rename stages or insert extra steps** to match their internal workflows, such as separating internal review from funder submission or distinguishing partial awards.

Even with custom names, keep the same general flow:

- Early interest (**Discovery**-type)
- Working the opportunity (**Pipeline**-type)
- Awaiting decision (**Submitted**-type)
- Awarded (**Won**-type)
- Spending and delivery (**Active Grant**-type)
- Closeout and reports (**Reporting**-type)
- Historical only (**Closed**-type)

This consistency ensures portfolio and compliance reporting continues to line up with how Grantmaster expects grants and projects to behave.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="How do lifecycle stages connect to projects and setup?">

Lifecycle stages interact with how you set up projects and link activity back to specific grants. In practice:

- During **Discovery and Pipeline**, you may sketch potential projects but avoid detailed setup.
- When a grant moves to **Won** and then **Active Grant**, you typically **create or finalize the project** that will receive expenses, time, and outputs.

For a step-by-step walkthrough of creating and configuring a project that ties into your grants, see [Set up your organization and first project](/help-center/guides/setup-project).

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="How do I decide when to close out a grant vs. keep it Active?">

Use these checks to decide:

- Keep the grant in **Active Grant** if teams are still charging time or expenses, or if deliverables are still in progress.
- Move the grant to **Reporting** once spending is complete and your main work is reconciling and reporting.
- Move the grant to **Closed** after you submit final reports and receive acceptance, and you do not expect further activity.

Managers and Admins should review Active and Reporting grants regularly to confirm which ones are ready to close so your active portfolio reflects current work accurately.

</Expandable>
