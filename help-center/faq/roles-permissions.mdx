## Roles and permissions overview

Grantmaster uses role-based access control so each person has the level of access they need without overexposing sensitive data or controls.

<Callout kind="info">

Follow least-privilege: hold the lowest role that still lets you do your work. If you need more access, request a role change instead of sharing accounts.

</Callout>

<ExpandableGroup>

<Expandable title="What are the core roles in Grantmaster?">

Grantmaster defines four customer-facing roles plus one platform-only role:

- **ADMIN** — Full control of the organization configuration.
- **MANAGER** — Oversight of projects and approvals.
- **MEMBER** — Day-to-day contributors submitting their own work.
- **AUDITOR** — Read-only access for review and export.

A separate **SUPER_ADMIN** role exists for platform operations and support. Customers do not assign or manage SUPER_ADMIN; it is restricted to the Grantmaster team.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="What can an ADMIN do?">

Admins manage the entire organization:

- Configure **org settings**, policies, and billing.
- Invite, remove, and update **user roles**.
- Set up and manage **projects and grants**.
- Control global workflows that affect how expenses and journals move through approvals.

If you see options to manage billing, organization-wide settings, or user roles, you are likely an ADMIN.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="What can a MANAGER do?">

Managers focus on operational oversight:

- Review and **approve or reject expenses** submitted by Members.
- Review and **approve journals or time entries** for their projects.
- Manage **projects and grants** they are responsible for, including assigning team members.
- View reporting for their projects without having full organization-level control.

Managers typically cannot change org-wide billing, policies, or user roles. If you need those capabilities, speak with an Admin.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="What can a MEMBER do?">

Members are individual contributors:

- **Submit their own expenses** and supporting documents.
- **Log journals or time entries** related to assigned projects or grants.
- View the status of their own submissions and approvals.

Members cannot approve other users' expenses or journals, modify org settings, or see sensitive financial details beyond what is required for their own work.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="What can an AUDITOR do?">

Auditors have read-only access optimized for oversight:

- View **all relevant records** such as projects, grants, expenses, and journals.
- **Export data** for external review or compliance.
- Run audit queries and reconcile records without changing underlying data.

Auditors cannot create, edit, approve, or delete records, and they cannot change org settings or user roles.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="What is SUPER_ADMIN and why do I not see it?">

SUPER_ADMIN is a platform-level role used by Grantmaster for operations and support. It is not a customer-facing role:

- Customers do **not** assign SUPER_ADMIN to their users.
- SUPER_ADMIN access is tightly controlled and audited by the Grantmaster team.
- When support staff use elevated access, it is for time-limited troubleshooting or maintenance.

If someone at your organization appears to have unusual access beyond ADMIN, contact support through your normal channel to confirm their role.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Why do I not see a project or grant I expect to have access to?">

If a project or grant is missing:

- You might not be **assigned** to that project as a Member or Manager.
- Your role may not allow viewing **all** projects by default.
- The project might not be created yet or is archived.

First, confirm with your internal Admin or project owner that:

- The project or grant exists.
- You are added to the correct project team.

If you are setting up a new project and do not see it after configuration, follow the steps in [Set up your organization and first project](/help-center/guides/setup-project) and verify your project setup matches your expected structure.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="How do I request a role change (for example, from MEMBER to MANAGER)?">

To request a role change:

1. Identify which role you need (for example, MANAGER to approve expenses for your team).
2. Contact your **organization Admin** using your standard internal process (IT ticket, manager request, or email).
3. Provide:
   - Your current role (if known).
   - The role you need.
   - The specific actions you need to perform (for example, approve journals for a project).

Only Admins in your organization can update roles. If you are unsure who that is, ask your manager or finance/operations lead.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="What should I do if I believe I have incorrect access?">

If you think your access is wrong (too much or too little):

1. Stop any action that might be impacted by incorrect permissions, especially approvals or configuration changes.
2. Capture a quick example:
   - A project you can or cannot see.
   - An action you can or cannot perform (for example, approving expenses).
3. Share this with your **Admin** and describe what you believe your access should be.

Admins can adjust your role or confirm that the current permissions are intentional. If your Admin suspects a system misconfiguration, they can escalate to Grantmaster support with details of your account and the affected projects or grants.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Why can I not approve expenses or journals?">

You likely do not have a role with approval permissions for that scope:

- **Managers** approve expenses and journals for their projects.
- **Admins** may be configured to approve at an org or grant level, depending on your setup.
- **Members** and **Auditors** cannot approve submissions.

Confirm:

- Your current role.
- That you are assigned as a Manager or approver on the specific project or grant.
- That the workflow for that project expects you as an approver.

If your role and assignment look correct but approvals still fail, your access issue might be related to sign-in or account configuration. Check [Grantmaster page will not load](/help-center/troubleshooting/install-error) for general troubleshooting or contact your Admin.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Does my role affect sign-in or account access?">

Roles control what you can do **after** you sign in. If you cannot sign in at all, the issue is separate from your permission level.

If you cannot access your account:

- Confirm you are using the correct email and authentication method.
- If you still cannot sign in, follow the guidance in [Help Center](/help-center), [Grantmaster page will not load](/help-center/troubleshooting/install-error), and [Troubleshoot sign-in problems](/help-center/troubleshooting/cant-sign-in), or contact your Admin for help.

Once you regain access, your existing role (ADMIN, MANAGER, MEMBER, or AUDITOR) will determine what you see and which actions you can perform.

</Expandable>

</ExpandableGroup>
